The main advantage of the insertion sort algorithm comes into play when lists are sorted or almost sorted. 
In these scenarios, insertion sorts time complexity is reduced to O(n) because of the comparative operation 
to it’s previous node allows the algorithm to stay out of an extra loop that would drive the time complexity to O(n^2).

The limitation comes in when working with large dataset that are unsorted. 
In these cases the algorithm needs to go into another comparative operator which drives time complexity to O(n^2).
